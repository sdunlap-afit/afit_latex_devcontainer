\abstract{
Accurate attitude determination is essential for the success of spacecraft missions, impacting various operational aspects such as communication, navigation, and scientific observation. This study investigates the effect of timing errors on the accuracy of attitude determination systems, specifically focusing on clock drift, timestamp inaccuracies, and measurement latency. The research evaluates the performance of different attitude estimation algorithms under varying conditions and identifies potential degradation in accuracy due to timing discrepancies. Simulations of a spacecraft equipped with a star tracker and a three-axis gyroscope emphasize the need for robust error mitigation strategies in attitude determination systems to enhance the reliability and efficiency of space missions.
}
